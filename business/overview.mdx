CalBudget Business is the business product lane for verified companies that want to audit SaaS spend, control renewals, and prove savings. It is separate from the consumer CalBudget calendar app.

<Warning>
  Do not use consumer CalBudget plan names for CalBudget Business. Free, Plus, and Navigator are consumer plans only.
</Warning>

## Who it is for

CalBudget Business is built for:

- Startup founders reducing burn
- Operations teams managing tools and renewals
- Bookkeepers turning transaction review into a review workflow
- Fractional CFOs running repeatable SaaS spend audits for clients

## What it helps teams do

CalBudget Business focuses on the SaaS spend loop:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Collect spend data">
    Connect or upload company spend data for review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Detect recurring SaaS vendors">
    Identify subscriptions, repeat charges, renewals, and vendor patterns.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach evidence">
    Add context, confidence, and supporting details to each opportunity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign action">
    Create savings opportunities with an owner, due date, approval path, and outcome tracking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Report savings">
    Review potential savings, confirmed savings, renewal risk, and completed actions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Business access

CalBudget Business is intended for verified business workspaces. Full activation should happen only after business access is approved.

After approval, your business workspace gives your team a shared place to review SaaS spend, renewals, evidence, and savings actions.

## What CalBudget Business does not do automatically

CalBudget Business should never imply that it cancels every SaaS product automatically. It creates evidence-backed workflows, reminders, instructions, and reports.

Sensitive actions require explicit human approval and should leave an audit trail.

## Keep it separate from consumer CalBudget

Consumer CalBudget helps individuals plan cash flow by date. CalBudget Business helps companies review SaaS spend and renewal risk.

The products have separate:

- Product surfaces
- Billing and subscription paths
- Workspace and role models
- Data privacy expectations
- Support and deletion expectations

Use CalBudget Business when talking about the company workspace product.
