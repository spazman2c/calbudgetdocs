---
title: "BIZ data boundaries"
description: "Keep CalBudget Business data, billing, integrations, and database access separate from the consumer CalBudget app."
---

CalBudget Business uses a separate product and data boundary from consumer CalBudget. This protects personal budget data, business workspace data, billing records, integrations, and audit trails from being mixed together.

<Warning>
  Do not import the consumer database client into BIZ code, and do not import the BIZ database client into consumer code.
</Warning>

## Product boundary

| Surface | Routes | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Consumer CalBudget | `/calendar`, `/debt`, `/billing`, `/subscribe`, `/support` | Personal calendar-first cash-flow planning. |
| CalBudget Business | `/business`, `/biz/*` | Verified business SaaS spend audit and renewal control. |

Consumer docs should use Free, Plus, and Navigator. Business docs should use CalBudget Business and business workspace language.

## Database boundary

| Area | Consumer CalBudget | CalBudget Business |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Database env var | `DATABASE_URL` | `BIZ_DATABASE_URL` |
| Schema | `src/db/schema.ts` | `src/db/biz/schema.ts` |
| Migrations | `src/db/migrations` | `src/db/biz/migrations` |
| DB client | `src/db` | `src/db/biz` |
| Drizzle config | Consumer Drizzle config | `drizzle.biz.config.ts` |

## Data that belongs in Business

Business workspaces, members, verification, SaaS vendors, business accounts, transactions, evidence, opportunities, cancellation workflows, billing records, integrations, reports, and audit events belong in the BIZ database.

They should not be stored in the personal CalBudget database.

## Data that belongs in consumer CalBudget

Personal accounts, personal transactions, categories, recurring series, bookmarks, debt planner data, consumer support tickets, consumer feedback, consumer onboarding events, and consumer billing belong in the consumer database.

They should not depend on the BIZ database.

## API boundary

Future Business APIs should live under `/api/biz/*`. In the current platform, no `/api/biz/*` routes exist.

Consumer APIs should continue to protect user-scoped personal data. Business APIs should protect workspace-scoped business data.

## Customer-facing implications

For customers, this means:

- A consumer CalBudget plan does not activate CalBudget Business.
- A CalBudget Business workspace does not change a consumer Free, Plus, or Navigator plan.
- Consumer account deletion is not the same as deleting a business workspace.
- Business verification, billing, and audit history should stay with the business workspace.

<Note>
  Shared presentation utilities are fine when they do not cross the data, billing, auth, or entitlement boundary.
</Note>
